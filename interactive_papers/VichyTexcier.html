ADVICE TO THE OCCUPIEE

1. Street peddlers offer them maps of Paris and conversation manuals; buses unload waves of them incessantly in front of Notre-Dame and the Pantheon; there isn’t one who doesn’t have his small camera glued to his eye. [They’re there, with a placid look, like children.] Yet don’t be mistaken: THEY ARE NOT TOURISTS.

2. They are conquerors. Be polite with them. But do not, in order to make yourself look good, go beyond their desires. No precipitation. In addition, they will not be grateful.

3. You do not know their language, or you have forgotten it. If one of them speaks to you in German, gesture that you don’t understand, and, without feeling guilty, continue on your way.

4. If they speak to you in French, do not feel obligated to show him a little bit of direction. They are not travelling companions.

5. If in the café, or in the restaurant, he tries to start a conversation, make him politely understand that what he has to say does not interest you at all.

6. If he asks you for a light, hand him your cigarette. Never, from time immemorial, has one ever refused a light – even to your most mortal enemy.

7. If they think that’s it’s crafty to spread defeatism in the heart of the citizenry by offering them concerts in our public spaces, you are not obliged to attend them. Stay at home or go to the countryside and listen to the birds.

8. Since you have been “occupied” they parade in your dishonor. Will you sit there and watch them? Occupy yourself with the storefront windows. It’s much more moving, because, at the rate their trucks fill up, you will soon not be able to find anything to buy.

9. Your suspender merchant thought that it was a good idea to put a sign up in his boutique: “Man Spricht Deutsch”; go to his neighbor, even if he appears to ignore the language of Goethe.

10. If you see a girl having a business conversation with one of them, don’t get offended. This boy will get his money’s worth—which is worthless. And tell yourself that 3/4s of French people would not show themselves with this girl, who is more delicate than the little blond of the Black Forest.

11. In front of the light-hearted gallantries of one of these women said to be “honest,” with one of your occupiers, remember that on the other side of the Rhine this pretty person would be publicly whipped. Now, while thinking of this, carefully remember the place and savor, in advance, your pleasure.

12. If by necessity, you need to address yourself to one of these bronze sentinels that guards the Kommandtur, don’t think that you need to take off your hat, like I’ve seen. Somberly raise your index finger to the height of your brim. Be economical with your manners.

13. It’s true. They know how to sing in unison and in tune. But it’s on command, like a breathing exercise. Here in France, the soldiers sing out of tune and rarely in time; but they ignore the singing labor duty. They sign whenever they please.

14. Reading newspapers in France has never been recommended for those who want to learn how to correctly express themselves in French. Today, it’s even better, the Parisian dailies are not longer even thought in French.

15. Abandoned by your TSF [radio], abandoned by your newspaper, abandoned by your party, far from your family and friends, learn how to think for yourself. But tell yourself that, in this continued desolation, the voice that pretends to give you courage is the voice of Dr. Goebbels. Abandoned spirit, beware of German propaganda!

16. They are very “chatty.” After patting the children on the head, they smile at the mother and soon groan at the fate of France. Then, the pitch follows: “Poor French, you have been led into a terrible war by a government of scoundrels in the pay of England”—and they recite their couplet to no matter who, about no matter what.
