Summary of the Forman Diary from September 1939

[Page 1] [Comments] POLISH Felt like trapped rat. Market day massacres. Russ[ia] want[s] Bessarabia, Hun[gar]y want[s] Transylv[ania]. Cav[alry] vs. mechan[ization]. French mad. Took no advice. Proud. Waited weeks before ask help, then too late. Would not even tell where front line was, much less show it. How could planes come to help if unknowing where to land. Also, no stores of ammunition for

[Page 2] [Comments] Fr[ench] plane guns and bomb-racks. Poles so reliant on cavalry, that they asked for no help for fear glory of German defeat might be stolen by Fr[ench] or Brit[ish]. Cavalry might have been valuable, if rain had come. Army surrounded most of Warsaw. Another ½ west of Lwów. Poles didn’t even have time to mob[ilize].

[Page 3] [Comments] Day after first call many returned, for had no uniforms or arms. Pol[ish] Army had no field wireless or even telephones. Consequently when spies cut few commercial telephone lines, High Command lost complete contact with armies. One general didn’t know where other was, and often where his own was.

[Page 4] [Comments] Smidgly-Ridz not a trained general, but a hero of Bolshevik War - - guerrilla fighting, etc. But so conceited that would take no advice from French. Consequently, strung his army along 1000 km of Germ. border. Germ. concentrated attacks at several points and broke thru. Poles

[Page 5] [Comments] should have retired to Vistula: (1) To shorten and straighten line to 500 km (2) To dig in for a strong stand. But Poles refused to give up any territory under any circumstances. Result, were out- flanked and surrounded, with not no strong second line to fall back on even if could retreat in

[Page 6] [Comments] time. Romania and other small countries refuse to profit by Poland’s fall. Still insist on complete neutrality – sepa – rately – refusing proferred Br. + Fr. help, for fear antagonizing Germ. Yet what will But will, like Poland, yell for help when too late, as

[Page 7] [Comments] Toute s’arrange a Romanie It. Feel that were forced To turn to G. because of Brit. opposition to Ethiop. Know that Germ., whom They hate, would xx them if join Hitler. Have good sense of humor. Laugh aloud at dwarfish Jap. diplomats in newark being received by It. officials. Significant that It. Controlled press speaks of “Russ. intervention” in Poland.

[Page 8] [Comments] It. definitely scared by Russ. angle in War. Revolution here before church would allow joining Unholy Alliance. Blame everything on Eden’s policy in Eth. which forced It. into arms of Hitler. 300,000 Fascist Militia received 8 lira/day, compared with ½ lira/day rec’d by regular troops. Agitation resulted in reducing Fas. Mil. Pay to 1 lira/day and raising reg./troops to same.

[Page 9] [Comments] Italians first knew of Germ.-Russ Pact only 8 hrs. before signing. Ciano Mad, because means end of Anti-Comintern Pact. Also double-cross. Were It. to join Germ. they would get worst win or lose. They’re are vulnerable to attack by air and sea. Even Franco annoyed at Hitler double-cross. Hitler told by Muss. thru Ciano that It. would support him in another blackmailing Munich.

[Page 10] [Comments] But if Hitler insisted on Poland attack, It. would not come in. Hit. then made deal with Russ. – who now also worried at possible xx with Germ. on their border. Are forced to mobilize to and worry about Hitler’s g wanting of Ukraine. It. Press, which is One of most contr- rolled in Europe, is Completely objective

[Page 11] [Comments] in reporting war. Absolutely no Opinionating, which is amazing. No chest-pounding. No mention of Axis. Are worried about Germ. pressure to supply materials, knowing Fr. + Eng. Watching closely, and if F.HE. suspect this will demand Italy come in for or against, or at least - - be blockaded. So hopes

[Page 12] [Comments]of Italy to profit by war thus blighted. Formerly many Germ. “technicians” here. Now none. Man in street glad. Hate Germans. Italy wants no war. Least jittery of all neutrals. If forced to make choice, more chance to join Allies than Hitler -- who’ll make Colony Of Italy. No single word from M. in 2 wks. Hope for ruin, Servants closing wind- ows. Sweltering

Blued flashlights. but bright moon floods city so easily spotted from air. V8 proti- eering under penalty of concentration camp. Everyone digs trenches. Men, women + child- ren. No. of hours record- ed in a pass-card given

[Page 21] [Comments] Perhaps they did have anti-aircraft guns. If so, still more disgraceful in not have ammunition. Jews will suffer most. 3 1/2 million of them. 10% pop. -- great- est proportion of all European countries. (Germany has only 1% Jews.) And, despite Poles protest that Jews have control of industry the Jews in Poland

[Page 22] [Comments] are of the lowest class. In Ghettos, bearded and booted, filth, and clad in long-gowned trad- itional garb. Well they knew what was in store for them. Yet what could they do? Where could they go? Were not permitted to travel. For that matter, all refugee travel was stopped after first few days.

[Page 23] [Comments] Gov't explained this by saying had to keep roads clear. Gov't radio also claimed fantastic successes . . such as capture of Konigsberg and half of E. Prussia. Also reports of Fr. “advancing another 80 km today beyond Rhine." Details given of “dampness” and such, in Siegfried Line which Fr. supposed to have taken.

[Page 24] [Comments] Gave out rumors that Hitler was abdicating. Govt. out to Rom. in scores airplanes. Germ. knew Poles unprepared for rapid mobilization. Thus plan was to drive thru with strong spear- head mechanized attacks without stop- ping to mop up or consolidate, which coupled with almost complete paralization of communications
